### Body 1(평양냉면을 둘러싼 논쟁 및 소개)

**<font color="RED">What is Pyongyang Naengmyeon?</font>** 
<span style="background:#d4b106">(평양냉면에 대한 부정적인 여론 사진)</span>
<span style="background:#d4b106">(평양냉면에 대한 긍정적인 여론 사진)</span>
평양냉면 is one of the most controversial foods in Korea. People either love it or hate it. For those who try it for the first time—or for those who simply don’t enjoy it—평양냉면 is often described as tasting like “mop water”.<span style="background:#d4b106">(대걸레 사진과 찌뿌린 얼굴 표정 사진)</span>
However, to it's fans, 평양냉면 is a special delicacy. They appreciate its subtle flavors, the meaty aroma of the clear broth, and the unique texture of the buckwheat<span style="background:#d4b106">(which is 메밀 in Korean / 메밀 사진)</span> noodles, which are slightly chewy and break apart as you eat them

<font color="RED">Comparing Pyongyang Naengmyeon and Hamheung Naengmyeon</font>
<span style="background:#d4b106">(평양냉면 vs 함흥냉면 사진)</span>
People often compare 평양냉면 with 함흥냉면, another type of cold noodle dish in Korea. While both are served cold, they are completely different in almost every other way.
The biggest differences are in the broth and the noodles. 평양냉면is usually made with buckwheat noodles and a light broth made from beef and dongchimi juice(동치미 사진). On the other hand, 함흥냉면 uses noodles made from sweet potato starch(고구마전분 사진), and it is served with a spicy red sauce instead of broth.

## Body 2(서북면옥의 역사)
<font color="RED">The History of 서북면옥  </font>
![[Pasted image 20241204211705.png|200]]
Now, let me tell you about the history of 서북면옥.  
This restaurant has been in business for **57 years**, opening its doors back in **1968**. The current owner, Ms. Lee Kyung-mi, took over the restaurant from her mother-in-law, who founded 서북면옥. Her mother-in-law was originally from 사리원, 황해 Province in where is now North Korea. After fleeing to South Korea, she opened the restaurant in its current location in 구의동.
<span style="background:#d4b106">(서북면옥의 오래된 내부사진 - 벽에 걸려있는 글귀들)</span>
As a restaurant with over 50 years of history, 서북면옥 has a lot of its old charm. The interior is small and simple, reflecting the humble beginnings of the restaurant. The atmosphere feels nostalgic, everything inside the restaurant shows the character of a long-standing, traditional eatery.

<font color="RED">Overall menu introduction</font>
Next, let me introduce the menu at 서북면옥. The restaurant serves various dishes such as 물냉면(cold noodles in broth), 비빔냉면(spicy cold noodles), 만두(dumplings). Like many other naengmyeon restaurants, they also serve 수육(boiled beef slices) and 편육(pressed pork slices).

Among these, the most famous dish is, of course, 물냉면 - which is 평양냉면. As you would expect from 평양냉면, it comes with a traditional setup: two slices of 편육, pickled radish, and half a boiled egg, served on top of buckwheat noodles in a beef-based broth.

The broth is light, clean, and free of any unpleasant smell. It’s not overly salty, making it refreshing and easy to enjoy. You can also taste the signature buckwheat flavor in the noodles, which becomes even stronger as you sip the broth or chew the noodles.

<font color="RED">Famous for Mandu, Too</font>  
In addition to Pyongyang Naengmyeon, Seobuk Myeonok is also famous for its **mandu**. The dumplings are simple and light, filled with pork, chives, tofu, and other basic ingredients. They are not overly seasoned, allowing the natural flavors of the ingredients to shine. Even the kimchi served here focuses on a mild, tangy flavor rather than being overly spicy or salty.

Overall, the food at Seobuk Myeonok reflects the philosophy written on the restaurant’s wall: “대미필담,” which means “Delicious flavors are inherently light and clean.” This phrase perfectly captures the essence of their dishes, which focus on simplicity and balance.
